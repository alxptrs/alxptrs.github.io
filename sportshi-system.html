Designing a Unified System
–

We launched the SportsHi MVP in late 2018. At that time, the idea of the product and company were still evolving. Over the course of the next year, design and engineering of new features was rapid and ad hoc. This lead to inconsistencies throughout the UI/UX. It was clear we needed an overarching system to guide our design.

In mid-2019, I began research on design systems and planned a course of action for implementation at SportsHi. Today, with an in-house engineering team, research and user testing, a clear vision of the product, and an active user-base, we are rebuilding the product from the ground-up with the SportsHi Design System keeping the digital experience consistent.

Inventory

After research, the first step was to conduct an inventory/internal audit of the app. It revealed inconsistencies in components, a varied palette with too many HEX codes, and contrasting UX patterns.

Screenshots, screenshots, screenshots!

Colors

After reviewing the app, I found some glaring problems which were preventing design consistency but also creating issues for developers. The biggest being, too many HEX codes and a color palette that lacked a proper variable system.

When reproaching the color palette, I reduced the number of colors used in the app to our primary and secondary color, blacks and grays for text and UI, background and overlays, and a few accent colors. There was now an easy variable system attached to each color group which gives ease to designers and developers. The introduction of gradations of hues and more grays made creating UI components much quicker and more visually consistent.
